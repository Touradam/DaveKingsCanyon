About the Land

Sierra Foothills. Highway Frontage. Park Country.

Set in the Sierra Nevada foothills at Dunlap, an unincorporated community in Fresno County, this parcel sits at roughly 1,900 feet of elevation on Highway 180. The route is known as the Gateway to the Sierras and marks the start of the 50-mile Kings Canyon Scenic Byway. Fresno is about 38 miles to the west. The entrance to Sequoia and Kings Canyon National Parks at Grant Grove is only 19 to 20 miles away.

The setting combines warm, dry Mediterranean summers with mild winters and occasional foothill snow. Oak woodlands, rolling terrain, and open mountain views surround the land. Giant Sequoia National Monument, the Kings River, Pine Flat Reservoir, and Wonder Valley are all close by, making this a natural stopping point for travelers headed into the parks.

Rare size, scenic character, direct highway frontage, and proximity to two national parks make this an outstanding candidate for tourism and hospitality investment.
