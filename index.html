About the Land

At the Snowline. On the Scenic Byway. Minutes from the Parks.

From Highway 180, the land opens onto more than four hundred acres of rolling Sierra Nevada foothills near Squaw Valley in unincorporated Fresno County. At roughly 4,500 feet of elevation, the parcel sits along the Kings Canyon Scenic Byway, the 50-mile route known as the Gateway to the Sierras. Fresno lies about 38 miles to the west. The entrance to Kings Canyon and Sequoia National Parks is only 7½ miles away.

Warm, dry Mediterranean summers give way to mild winters with occasional foothill snow. Oak woodlands, chaparral slopes, specimen manzanita, and massive granite outcrops define the terrain. Aerial views reveal winding access roads, open gravel clearings, and ridge lines that look out over layered valleys. Just beyond the property: General Grant Grove and the Nation’s Christmas Tree; Kings Canyon, one of the deepest canyons in the country, with the Kings River, Boyden Caverns, and Cedar Grove; and Sequoia National Park, home to the General Sherman Tree, 36 miles away on the Generals Highway.

Recreational zoning, five miles of highway frontage, and proximity to two national parks position this land as a credible candidate for tourism, hospitality, or estate development.

Development Concepts

Four Paths for 410 Acres

Sample concepts illustrate what this scale of land can support. These are starting points for vision, not finished plans. Select any rendering to view it full size.

This sale includes three tax lots: 105 acres zoned recreational; 68 acres zoned recreational with a two-acre carve-out for the Snowline Lodge, which has a long-term lease until 2050; and 240 acres zoned RC 40, which allows for six home sites on 40 acres each.

Grand Resort

Kings Canyon National Park draws more than 800,000 visitors each year, enough traffic to support a large destination resort. The National Park Service has removed private lodges within the park and replaced them with rustic accommodations. Seller materials describe this as rare private recreational-zoned land with the attributes needed for a luxury complex.

The property offers buildable acreage, miles of highway frontage, proximity to the park entrance (7.5 miles), access to a known aquifer, and electrical power in place. A destination resort here would face limited nearby competition.

Glamping Resort

The same secluded layout, elevated along the ridge with valley views, upgraded with private tent-style suites. Each unit offers a king bed, a private bath, and hotel-level comfort in an outdoor setting framed by oak woodland and open sky.

Campground

Individual campsites can nest into brush and manzanita, screened from one another so guests enjoy privacy, quiet, and a direct connection to the landscape. The on-site gravel clearing offers a natural, level foundation for grouped or dispersed sites.

Combination: Small Campground + Luxury Caretaker’s Residence

For buyers seeking a manageable business paired with a private residence, this concept combines a small five-to-ten dry-site, self-service campground with a caretaker’s home that carries no legal size limit. Dry camping means no electrical, water, or sewer hookups. Self-contained campers need only a level, safe place to park for the night. Payment can be handled online or through a drop-box. No campfires would be allowed. Maintenance stays simple: routine cleaning around the sites and occasional trash pickup. The campground would sit near the highway entrance, while the residence could rise anywhere else on the property, including atop the ridge overlooking the San Joaquin Valley.

Development Potential

Scale, Access, and a Landscape That Sells Itself

Four hundred ten acres and five miles of frontage on one of California’s most scenic highways put scale, visibility, and natural character in a single holding. The photos and renderings here are entry points, not ceilings. Every concept builds on the same strengths: privacy within oak woodlands, granite ridges with valley views, specimen manzanita, and a location between Fresno and Sequoia and Kings Canyon National Parks.

The land can support eco-tourism, upscale glamping, a destination resort, a private estate with guest accommodations, corporate retreats, or gateway lodging for park visitors. For investors and developers, highway exposure, scenic byway traffic, and nearby recreation destinations combine into a long-term opportunity worth serious study.
